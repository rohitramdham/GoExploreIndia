Qutb Minar, Delhi

About Qutb Minar

The Qutb Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Delhi, India. It is a 73-meter tall tapering tower of five storeys, with a 14.3-meter base diameter, reducing to 2.7 meters at the top. The construction of the Qutb Minar was started by Qutb-ud-din Aibak in 1193 and completed by his successor, Iltutmish, in 1220. It is one of the finest examples of Indo-Islamic architecture.
