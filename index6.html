Hawa Mahal, Jaipur

About Hawa Mahal

The Hawa Mahal, also known as the "Palace of Winds," is a stunning pink sandstone palace located in Jaipur, India. Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, the palace is renowned for its unique five-story exterior with 953 small windows called 'jharokhas' that allow the cool air to pass through, providing a natural air conditioning effect. The palace was designed to enable the royal women to observe street festivals while remaining unseen from the outside.
